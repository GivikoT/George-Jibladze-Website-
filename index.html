George Jibladze:

Honoring the Legacy of a Georgian National Ballet Dancer

George Jibladze was a highly accomplished dancer in the State Honored Academic Ensemble of Georgian National Dance. He was known for his exceptional performance style and contributed significantly to the ensemble's creative success for over 20 years. Jibladze was awarded the honorary titles of Honored Artist of the Republic and People's Artist of the Republic for his outstanding service to the development of Georgian national choreography.

Biography

George Jibladze was born on March 31, 1934 in Tbilisi. He mastered the art of Georgian national dance in the choreographic ensemble at the Tbilisi Production Cooperative House under the leadership of the Honored Artist of the Republic, Mikheil Shubashikeli. From 1953 to 1973, he worked as a dancer in the State Honored Academic Ensemble of Georgian National Dance under the leadership of People's Artists of the Soviet Union Nino Ramishvili and Iliko Sukhishvili. He achieved real professional perfection in the academic ensemble. Giorgi Jibladze was always distinguished by his own performance style. An excellent performer of "Mtiuluri" (mountain dance), he showed his indomitable temperament brilliantly while performing various complex tricks.

For 20 years, one of the leading dancers of the ensemble, has been dedicatedly involved in the beloved collective and contributed modestly to its creative success. For his active participation in the development of Georgian national choreography and outstanding service, George Jibladze was awarded with the honorary title of Honored Artist of the Republic in 1967, and People's Artist of the Republic in 1971 by the decree of the Presidium of the Supreme Council of Georgia. He was awarded with multiple certificates and medals of the Government of Georgia. Giorgi Jibladze passed away on April 12, 1994, and was laid to rest in the Saburtalo Pantheon in Tbilisi.
